Sandra Oh's Family

Sandra Oh is a Canadian-American actress, who was born on July 20, 1971 in Ontario, Canada. Her parents are South Korean immigrants, Jeon Young-nam, and Oh Jun-su. Oh's siblings include her brother, Ray, and sister, Grace, who all grew up in a christian household.

Sandra Oh's Early Life

Sandra Oh did many things in her early life. When she was 10, she starred in a class musical called The Canada Goose, later on, she was also the elected student council president, and she was the founder of BASE (Borden Active Students for the Environment), which was an environmental club that lead a campaign against styrofoam cup use. Furthermore, Oh rejected a four-year scholarship to Carleton University, and paid her own way to The National Theatre School of Canada.

Sandra Oh's Career History

Sandra Oh has starred in many films and movies. Two of the most popular ones are the movies Sideways and The Diary of Evelyn Lau, which Oh considers as the best ones she has made. Oh is also best known for her role as Cristina Yang in the medical hit drama Grey's Anatomy, and Eve Polastri in Killing Eve.

Apart from acting, Sandra Oh was also the executive producer of Umma, 6 episodes of The Chair, and 8 episodes of Killing Eve. Oh was also the producer of window horses, the co-executive producer of 8 episodes of Killing Eve, and the associate producer for 8 episodes of Killing Eve.
